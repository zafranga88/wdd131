Seasonal Hiking Guide

Summer (December - February)

Summer is the peak hiking season in Patagonia, offering relatively stable weather and longer daylight hours. Temperatures typically range from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F), making it comfortable for hiking.

What to Expect:

  • Crowded popular trails and fuller accommodation
  • Strong winds, particularly in the afternoons
  • Beautiful wildflowers in bloom
  • Greater wildlife activity

Recommended Gear:

  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
  • Lightweight, moisture-wicking clothing
  • Windproof and waterproof outer layers
  • Insect repellent

Fall (March - May)

Fall brings spectacular autumn colors to Patagonia's forests, with the famous lenga trees turning brilliant shades of red and gold. Temperatures begin to drop, ranging from 5°C to 15°C (41°F to 59°F).

What to Expect:

  • Fewer crowds on trails
  • Stunning fall foliage, especially in April
  • Increasing precipitation
  • Shorter daylight hours

Recommended Gear:

  • Warmer layers (medium-weight base layers, fleece)
  • Waterproof hiking boots
  • Rain gear
  • Gloves and warm hat

Winter (June - August)

Winter in Patagonia brings snow to many trails, with temperatures often dropping below freezing. Temperatures typically range from -5°C to 5°C (23°F to 41°F), though they can be much colder with wind chill.

What to Expect:

  • Many trails and facilities closed due to snow
  • Very few visitors
  • Short daylight hours (around 8 hours)
  • Potential for severe weather

Recommended Gear:

  • Winter hiking boots and gaiters
  • Thermal base layers
  • Heavy insulation (down jacket)
  • Winter hiking equipment (crampons, ice axe) for some trails
  • Emergency shelter and communication device

Spring (September - November)

Spring brings a reawakening to Patagonia, with melting snow feeding waterfalls and rivers. Temperatures begin to warm, ranging from 5°C to 15°C (41°F to 59°F), though early spring can still see winterlike conditions.

What to Expect:

  • Variable conditions, from snow to rain to sun
  • Muddy trails in some areas
  • Fewer crowds than summer
  • Beautiful spring flowers beginning to bloom

Recommended Gear:

  • Layering system for variable temperatures
  • Waterproof hiking boots
  • Gaiters for muddy or snowy conditions
  • Rain gear
  • Trekking poles for stream crossings
